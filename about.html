Current Focus

If my career were a book, this current chapter would be called "The Great Exploration." I am a massive tech enthusiast with core interests in deep learning, machine learning, software engineering, and operating systems. To figure out where I want to apply these skills long-term, I’m actively testing the waters between industry and academia. Currently, I am leaning into the research world as a RISE-Globalink Research Internship Scholarship Recipient at the University of Calgary. My project focuses on learning representations of genomics data to characterize biology and predict diseases. It is challenging, fascinating, and giving me the perfect space to learn, grow, and figure out exactly what the future holds for me.

Technical Direction

I am constantly deepening my technical knowledge and abilities through personal projects and my homelab. When it comes to writing code, I mostly enjoy C++ programming, which I find the more entertaining and natural for me, though I also really like the flexibility and abstraction that the Python libraries offer. Right now, I have in plan to work on the MentOS and Linux From Scratch projects to deepen my kernel and operating systems development skills, while also building various projects in C++ or Rust to explore the capabilities of both languages. Beyond software, my homelab currently consists of a Raspberry Pi 5 running OMV (OpenMediaVault) a dedicated operating system that turns the board into a homemade network-attached server. Setting it up was great, but I had to research a lot about security and network security in order to keep my personal documents safe from any unforeseen attacks.

Community & Leadership

When I step away from my technical projects, I am deeply passionate about community, leadership, and sharing knowledge. A major milestone for me was being involved in the IBM Z Student Ambassador program, which opened the doors for me to be a speaker at an official IBM event at their headquarters in Boeblingen, Germany, and ultimately helped me land my internship at Living Mainframe right there in Boeblingen. I also really enjoy breaking down complex concepts to help others succeed, which led me to become a Teaching Assistant for the Elements of Calculus class. On a more global scale, I love connecting with people from different backgrounds and have been involved in four different Erasmus+ projects. Working as both a member and a coordinator, I helped organize international camps alongside Landessportjugend Sachsen-Anhalt that brought together participants from seven different countries.
