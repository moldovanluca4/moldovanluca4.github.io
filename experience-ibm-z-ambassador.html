The Experience Behind the Role

When I first joined the IBM Z community as a member, I had almost no understanding of the enterprise computing industry or the scale of the workloads it supports. I quickly discovered that IBM mainframes are not just legacy systems, but some of the most robust and reliable technologies powering critical parts of the global economy. Learning how much of the world depends on these systems fascinated me and motivated me to work hard, understand the field deeply, and help promote it to other students.

That motivation eventually led me to become an IBM Z Student Ambassador. Through the program, I developed a stronger understanding of enterprise computing, mainframe technology, and the importance of building technical communities around continuous learning. I wanted to make this field more approachable for other students by organizing workshops, facilitating events, answering questions, and creating spaces where people could explore IBM Z together.

One of the most meaningful moments of this journey was presenting at IBM headquarters in Boeblingen, Germany. A huge thank you goes to Joris Mertens for his support and guidance throughout that experience. It was my first industry event, and speaking in front of IBMers and industry professionals as a 19-year-old student was definitely not easy. Looking back, however, I realize that with commitment, curiosity, and consistent effort, it is possible to grow quickly and achieve meaningful things even in a short period of time.

During the three days I spent at IBM headquarters, I had the chance to see what day-to-day life looks like inside a major technology company with hundreds of employees. I met many professionals, learned from their experiences, and gained a clearer picture of how a tech giant operates from the inside. I also had the pleasure of visiting the IBM Museum, where we were taken through the history of IBM, from early computing machines to modern enterprise systems.

More than just a title, the ambassador experience showed me how powerful technical communities can be. People learn faster when they share knowledge, ask questions openly, and see a path from curiosity to real-world systems work. I will always be happy to return, connect with people in the mainframe community, and continue learning more about the world of enterprise computing.
